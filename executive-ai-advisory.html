01Executive AI advisory

Private counsel for the decisions only you can make.

Where to deploy AI. What to spend. What to keep human. Ash Williams advises Australian CEOs and directors on the calls that decide whether AI compounds value or burns capital — direct, private, one-on-one.

What the counsel covers
01The counsel

Four kinds of counsel. One advisor.

Every engagement is direct with Ash. No account managers, no juniors, no deck factory — advice grounded in systems he has designed and operated himself.

  • Opportunity review

    A disciplined read of where AI pays inside your business — across customers, team, operations and growth — and where it won't. Ranked, costed and argued, not brainstormed.

  • One-on-one CEO mentoring

    Private sessions that sharpen your judgement, your language and your confidence — so you can question vendors properly, brief the board plainly and lead AI from the chair.

  • Investment & deployment roadmap

    Each opportunity weighed for value, feasibility and sequence, with the investment stated in full. You decide with the numbers in front of you.

  • Standing counsel

    As systems go live and scale, Ash stays retained — pressure-testing decisions, reading vendor claims and keeping the program honest.

02The difference

Counsel from the person who builds it.

Vendors sell tools. Consultancies sell decks. You need someone accountable for what happens after the advice.

AIKING's counsel rests on a decade of designing and operating the kinds of systems it recommends. When Ash puts an opportunity on your priority map, he can also architect it, deploy it and govern it — which changes what gets recommended in the first place.
