## Official YouTube findings

From YouTube's own docs:

- YouTube says viewers are matched to videos they are most likely to watch and enjoy, not pushed purely because a channel is big.
- Videos are ranked by performance and viewer personalization: viewer history, topic relevance, watch/search history, and engagement/performance.
- YouTube's advice is essentially: know the audience, make what they actually watch, and recommendations follow when people respond.
- Title/thumbnail changes matter only because they change how viewers react to the video, not because the edit itself magically re-ranks the upload.
- Analytics that matter early: impressions, click-through rate, average view duration, retention, subscribers gained, likes, shares, and Shorts engaged views.
- Shorts views changed in 2025: starts/replays count as views, but `Engaged views` remains the stronger metric for comparing whether people actually kept watching.

## Strategic verdict

AIKING should launch as a **B2B trust + proof channel**, not a creator entertainment channel.

The zero-follower strategy is not to chase subscribers first. The strategy is:

1. Publish clear authority videos so the channel does not look empty.
2. Use Shorts to test hooks and pull in cold attention.
3. Use public mini-audits to show proof and create local relevance.
4. Convert attention into `DM AUDIT` / `aiking.info` / booked diagnosis calls.
5. Measure leads and conversations, not vanity subscribers.

## Measurement plan

For the first 30 days, ignore monetization and vanity numbers.

Track:

- Videos posted.
- Shorts engaged views.
- Average view duration / retention.
- Impressions and CTR for longform.
- Subscribers gained per video.
- Comments/DMs/questions.
- aiking.info clicks.
- Audit enquiries.

Decision rule:

- If a topic gets attention but no business relevance, use it sparingly.
- If a topic gets lower views but business-owner interest, keep making it.
- One real audit conversation is worth more than 10,000 random AI-tool views.
