Sky App — Privacy Policy

Private by design.

This policy explains what the public Sky iPhone app processes, why it is needed, which service providers are involved, and how you can delete your account.

Effective 24 July 2026

What Sky collects.

When you use Sign in with Apple, Sky receives Apple’s stable account identifier and an authorization code. Sky does not request your Apple name or email address. The service creates a random internal account ID and an App Account Token used to bind your Apple subscription to that account.

The server stores a one-way keyed representation of the Apple account identifier, the internal account ID, App Account Token, an encrypted Apple refresh token when Apple supplies one, subscription-entitlement status, and limited voice-session usage events needed to enforce account and service limits.

Realtime voice processing.

When you intentionally start a voice conversation, microphone audio and session content are transmitted to OpenAI to generate the realtime response. Sky’s public service does not store raw audio or conversation transcripts after the live provider session ends.

Do not share passwords, payment-card details, recovery codes or other highly sensitive credentials in a voice conversation.

How information is used.

  • Authenticate your account and keep the app session secure.
  • Verify, restore and enforce the Apple subscription.
  • Create the realtime voice session you request.
  • Apply per-account and service-wide usage limits before provider costs are incurred.
  • Detect failures, abuse and security incidents and provide support.

Service providers.

Apple provides account authentication, App Store distribution and subscription billing. OpenAI processes live voice and session content to generate responses. Cloudflare provides protected network routing. These providers process information under their own terms and privacy policies. Sky does not sell personal information and does not use account data for third-party advertising.

Storage and security.

The iPhone stores the short-lived Sky account session in Apple Keychain. Provider and Apple server credentials remain on the backend and are not shipped in the app. Account and entitlement data are separated from the private owner-only edition of Sky; public accounts cannot access its memory, tools or continuity.

Deletion and retention.

You can delete your Sky account from the Account menu in the app. Deletion revokes Apple access when a revocable token is available, removes the server-side account, entitlement and usage records, and invalidates existing Sky sessions. Deleting the Sky account does not cancel an Apple subscription; subscriptions are managed separately in Apple Account settings.

Operational records are retained while needed to provide and protect the service. Where law requires information to be preserved, it may be retained only for that required period.

Your choices and contact.

You may stop microphone access in iOS Settings, manage or cancel your subscription through Apple, or delete the Sky account in the app. For privacy questions or access and correction requests, email ash@aiking.info.

Policy changes.

This page may be updated when Sky’s features, providers or legal obligations change. The effective date above identifies the current version.
