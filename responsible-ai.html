Responsible AI

Capable systems. Accountable people.

AIKING's position is simple. AI should extend what your organisation can do — it should never move accountability away from the leaders and domain experts who own the outcome.

Core principles.

  • Human oversight remains explicit for high-stakes judgement, commitments and actions that carry reputational weight.
  • Agent authority is bounded — by role, by data, by tool access and by escalation path.
  • Systems are evaluated before deployment and monitored after it.
  • Outputs are auditable enough for leaders to understand both decisions and failures.
  • Data minimisation and permission design are built into the architecture — not added later.

In practice.

These principles take working form inside an engagement: authority matrices, evaluation plans, red-team testing, approval gates, monitoring cadence and incident review paths.

This statement describes the standard AIKING applies to the systems it builds and operates. The full control model — approval paths, access controls, evaluation and accountability — is set out on the Governance & Control page. Questions about this statement: ash@aiking.info.
