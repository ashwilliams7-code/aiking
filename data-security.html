Data & Security

Security as a design decision.

AIKING treats data access as an implementation design decision, not an afterthought. What a system may touch, who approves it and how it is monitored are settled before anything is built.

Initial enquiries

The enquiry form on this site prepares an email draft in your own mail client. Nothing you type is stored by the site. Use the form for high-level context only.

Do not send passwords, credentials, regulated data or confidential documents through the enquiry form. Secure channels for sensitive material are established once an engagement is scoped.

Implementation scoping

Before build work begins, the security posture of every system is worked through and agreed:

  • Data sources and access are identified before build work.
  • Permission boundaries are defined for people, agents and integrations.
  • Human approval gates are documented for sensitive workflows.
  • Model, vendor and tool choices are reviewed against risk and operational need.
  • Audit trails, logging expectations and escalation paths are agreed where relevant.

Security documentation

Detailed handling requirements, access controls and confidentiality commitments are captured in the engagement agreement or statement of work — in writing, before work begins.
