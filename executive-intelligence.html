Intelligence lineage

The named intelligence systems behind AIKING.

Before AI agents became a market trend, Ash had been building, breaking and refining named intelligence layers for more than a decade. This is the public-safe lineage: what each system was for, and what it taught the current AIKING operating model.

Zefron

Role: Early coding and agentic experimentation layer.

Use case: Testing how AI could support building, reasoning and execution before the tooling was mature.

Atlas

Role: Memory, planning and structured agent-workspace foundation.

Use case: Holding project context, decomposing work, coordinating plans and proving that AI needed operating memory, not just chat.

Sky v1

Role: First serious personal AI assistant and companion operating model.

Use case: Day-to-day support, context recall, task assistance, personality shaping and the first version of a persistent second brain.

Galaxy

Role: Expanded multi-agent and tooling experimentation.

Use case: Stress-testing multi-agent workflows, creative systems, research loops and broader tool-connected intelligence.

Sky v2

Role: Current refined personal AI assistant, second brain and coordination system.

Use case: Memory, voice, websites, research, content, automations, tools and real-world execution across Ash’s work.

Yogi

Role: Private engineering and coding-orchestration lane.

Use case: Turning strategy into software work through planning, implementation support and coordinated agent execution.

Client intelligence catalogue

Client-ready intelligence systems AIKING can build around real business work.

The lineage becomes useful when it is applied to business workflows: where information arrives, what needs judgement, what should be drafted, what must be escalated and what leaders need to see.

Lead Response Intelligence

Use case: Capture new enquiries from web forms, email, ads or DMs and respond before the opportunity goes cold.

Creates: qualification notes, reply drafts, booking prompts, urgency flags and human handoff.

Follow-Up & Quote Intelligence

Use case: Stop warm leads, open quotes and unfinished conversations from disappearing inside inboxes or CRMs.

Creates: chase sequences, quote-status summaries, objection notes, reminders and escalation points.

Sales Inbox Intelligence

Use case: Turn a messy sales inbox into a prioritised command view for owner-led teams and sales operators.

Creates: triage labels, response drafts, next-step recommendations, risk flags and daily pipeline briefings.

Client Onboarding Intelligence

Use case: Keep new clients moving through intake, documents, expectations, scheduling and internal handover.

Creates: onboarding checklists, missing-information prompts, status summaries and team handover notes.

Executive Decision Briefings

Use case: Give leaders concise context before decisions instead of making them search across tools and threads.

Creates: decision briefs, trade-off summaries, assumptions, risks, recommended next actions and owner notes.

Operations Intelligence

Use case: Surface bottlenecks, exceptions, overdue work and recurring friction across delivery or admin workflows.

Creates: operating signals, exception reports, bottleneck maps, escalation triggers and improvement notes.

Reporting Intelligence

Use case: Convert raw activity, spreadsheets and system exports into leadership-readable reporting.

Creates: weekly summaries, KPI narratives, variance explanations, risk commentary and board-ready updates.

Knowledge Base Intelligence

Use case: Make policies, SOPs, notes, documents and internal knowledge searchable without exposing everything to everyone.

Creates: retrieval systems, answer drafts, source-linked summaries, permission boundaries and update workflows.

Market & Competitor Intelligence

Use case: Monitor competitors, offers, trends, customer language and market shifts without manual research drag.

Creates: market briefs, competitor watchlists, positioning notes, opportunity alerts and content angles.

Content & Campaign Intelligence

Use case: Turn sales conversations, founder insight, FAQs and proof points into sharper marketing assets.

Creates: content briefs, campaign hooks, repurposed posts, audience objections and publishing calendars.

Revenue Recovery Intelligence

Use case: Identify missed calls, delayed replies, no-shows, dead CRM stages and unchased revenue opportunities.

Creates: leak maps, recovery lists, reactivation prompts, follow-up plans and owner-level visibility.

Governance & Risk Intelligence

Use case: Keep AI systems useful without letting them make uncontrolled client, money or reputation decisions.

Creates: approval gates, audit notes, sensitive-action flags, evaluation criteria and human oversight cadence.
